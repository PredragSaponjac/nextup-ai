02 Funding research · grants
SBIR/STTRState & foundationsFreshness-verified12-month plan

GrantSignal — non-dilutive funding, deep-researched

GrantSignal finds verifiably-active US non-dilutive funding for a specific venture. It infers the topic, stage and applicant model, then searches the right federal programs (SBIR/STTR, NOFOs), state and regional bodies, and private foundations — and ruthlessly cuts the big-looking programs that are structurally dead or a stage/applicant mismatch. Every opportunity is confirmed against an official source within 30 days, ranked in tiers by fit and timing, and delivered with an entity strategy and a 12-month application calendar. It spans biomedical, devices, digital health, ag, materials, energy/climate, defense dual-use and IT/AI — without forcing one field's logic onto another.

  • HowInfer topic / stage / applicant → search federal + state + foundations → verify currency → tier & sequence
  • DisciplineOfficial-source freshness (≤30 days) · expired or unannounced cycles are never ranked
  • OutputA tiered shortlist with deadlines · entity-structure advice · a 12-month sequencing calendar
  • ProvesRigorous, freshness-checked research that turns "find me grants" into an actionable plan

09 Automated research · options
Claude Opus670+ tickersSelf-auditingS&P-500 backtest

ORCA — an options research desk that runs itself

ORCA runs a two-stage pipeline on its own every trading morning. A quant scanner sweeps 670+ optionable stocks for gaps between the volatility the market is pricing and what each stock is actually doing — using several realized-vol estimators, an IV-rank from its own growing history, 25-delta skew and a vol cone — then a Claude analyst reads 25+ news, RSS and weather feeds, names the catalyst, and writes ranked trade ideas with exact strikes. Every idea is logged, snapshotted daily, and re-graded a week later by a second AI; a separate weekly engine runs a full S&P-500 scan and backtest on top.

  • HowScanner → Claude analyst → trade logger → weekly AI scorecard + S&P-500 backtest
  • Datayfinance chains · 9 news/RSS feeds · NWS weather · Polygon option history
  • Scale670+ tickers daily, on a SQLite IV history that sharpens as it grows
  • ProvesA complete find → decide → log → self-audit loop, fully unattended
10 Physical commodities · valuation
Crude & RINsDeterministicFail-safe gating

Hermes — a crude & RIN optionality desk

Hermes is a desk-grade engine for real commodity decisions — crude oil carry, crush and freight economics, and renewable-fuel (RIN) obligation trades under EPA rules. The numbers are computed deterministically, never guessed; AI agents scout live public data, value each position, and a skeptic layer audits the result. Its signature is refusal — in live mode it won't recommend anything until verified inputs exist — and it turns its findings into a clean written market brief.

  • HowData scout → deterministic valuer → skeptic QA → ranker → written desk memo
  • DataFRED (WTI / Brent / SOFR) · EPA renewable-volume obligations
  • SafeguardVerified-true/false gates on every input · auto-veto on missing data
  • ProvesRigor and restraint — it says "I can't" rather than bluffing
